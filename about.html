Six Hills village history

A place shaped by heritage, trade, and community.

Six Hills village has long stood as a distinctive landmark in Nottinghamshire, known for its strong sense of place and its links to the wider story of the region. Its name reflects the character of the landscape, where hills and open country have shaped settlement, travel, and local identity for generations.

Historically, the area has been important for agriculture, movement, and community life, with villages and rural routes helping connect people across the countryside. That heritage continues to matter today, as the village remains a symbol of continuity, resilience, and local pride.

The importance of Six Hills lies not only in its history, but in its enduring role as a place where the past still informs the present. It is a reminder that small communities often carry the deepest stories and strongest traditions.
